AP US History — Unit 1: Period 1 (1491–1607)

Pre-Columbian North America

  • Diverse Native societies organized around geography: Eastern Woodlands, Great Plains, Southwest, Pacific Northwest, Mississippi Valley
  • Mississippian culture: large mound-building cities (Cahokia), complex trade networks, maize-based agriculture
  • Southwest: Pueblo peoples (Anasazi/Ancestral Puebloans) developed permanent adobe settlements, irrigation
  • Great Plains: nomadic buffalo hunters; Eastern Woodlands tribes used farming and hunting
  • Most societies were kin-based, often matrilineal; land was communal, not privately owned

European Motivations for Exploration

The 3 G's: God (spread Christianity), Glory (national prestige), Gold (economic gain)
  • Ottoman Empire blocked overland trade routes to Asia → incentive to find sea routes
  • Technological advances: caravel, magnetic compass, astrolabe, Mercator maps
  • Portugal: led early exploration along Africa's coast; Vasco da Gama reached India (1498)
  • Spain: Columbus reached Caribbean (1492); Treaty of Tordesillas (1494) divided New World between Spain and Portugal
  • Reconquista mindset shaped Spanish conquest: military subjugation of non-Christians

The Columbian Exchange

Americas to Europe/Africa/AsiaEurope/Africa/Asia to Americas
Maize, potatoes, tomatoes, tobacco, cacao, squash, beansWheat, sugar, rice, cattle, horses, pigs, smallpox, measles, influenza
  • European diseases killed an estimated 50–90% of the Native population within a century of contact
  • Horses transformed Plains tribes' culture (hunting, warfare) after 1500s
  • Sugar cultivation in the Caribbean drove the transatlantic slave trade
  • New crops (potatoes, maize) fed population booms in Europe and Africa

Spanish Colonization

  • Conquistadors: Cortes defeated Aztec Empire (1521); Pizarro conquered Inca (1532)
  • Encomienda system: Spanish granted Native labor; brutal, led to mass death
  • Bartolome de las Casas: priest who condemned encomienda abuses; argued for Native rights
  • New Laws (1542) attempted reform; replaced with African slave labor over time
  • Mestizo population emerged from intermarriage; complex racial hierarchy (casta system)
  • Spanish established missions in Florida (1565) and New Mexico to convert Natives
