Calculus 2 — Unit 1: Applications of the Definite Integral

6.1 Finding Volumes Using Slices

The idea: slice a solid into thin cross-sections perpendicular to an axis, compute each cross-section's area \(A(x)\), and integrate.

\[ V = \int_a^b A(x)\, dx \]

Disk Method (region rotated about the \(x\)-axis, no gap):

\[ V = \pi \int_a^b [f(x)]^2\, dx \]

Washer Method (region between two curves rotated about the \(x\)-axis):

\[ V = \pi \int_a^b \left([f(x)]^2 - [g(x)]^2\right) dx \qquad \text{where } f(x) \ge g(x) \ge 0 \]
  • \(f(x)\) is the outer radius, \(g(x)\) is the inner radius
  • Rotating about the \(y\)-axis: integrate with respect to \(y\), express \(x = h(y)\)
  • Rotating about a horizontal line \(y = k\): radii become \(f(x) - k\) and \(g(x) - k\) (adjust signs carefully)
  • Cross-sections of known shape (square, triangle, semicircle): find \(A(x)\) from the geometry, then integrate directly

6.2 Finding Volumes Using Cylindrical Shells

The shell method wraps thin cylindrical shells around the axis of rotation instead of slicing perpendicular to it. Useful when the washer method requires two integrals or awkward expressions.

\[ V = 2\pi \int_a^b x\, f(x)\, dx \qquad \text{(rotation about the } y\text{-axis, region above } x\text{-axis)} \]
\[ V = 2\pi \int_a^b (\text{radius})(\text{height})\, dx \]
  • Radius: distance from the shell to the axis of rotation
  • Height: value of the function (or difference of functions) at that \(x\)
  • Rotating about the \(x\)-axis: integrate with respect to \(y\); radius = \(y\), height = \(h(y)\)
  • Rotating about \(x = k\): radius becomes \(|x - k|\)
SituationPreferred Method
Rotating about \(x\)-axis, functions of \(x\)Disk/Washer (integrate in \(x\))
Rotating about \(y\)-axis, functions of \(x\)Shells (integrate in \(x\)) -- often easier
Boundary expressed as \(x = g(y)\)Disk/Washer in \(y\)

6.3 Arc Length and Surface Area

Arc Length of \(y = f(x)\) on \([a,b]\):

\[ L = \int_a^b \sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2}\, dx \]

For \(x = g(y)\) on \([c,d]\):

\[ L = \int_c^d \sqrt{1 + [g'(y)]^2}\, dy \]

Surface Area of Revolution about the \(x\)-axis:

\[ S = 2\pi \int_a^b f(x)\sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2}\, dx \qquad (f(x) \ge 0) \]

About the \(y\)-axis:

\[ S = 2\pi \int_a^b x\sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2}\, dx \]
  • Arc length integrals are often difficult to evaluate in closed form; substitution or numerical methods may be needed
  • The factor \(2\pi r\) in the surface area formula is the circumference of the ring at each point

6.4 Moments and Centers of Mass

The center of mass \((\bar{x}, \bar{y})\) is the balance point of a lamina (thin flat plate) with density \(\rho\).

For a lamina bounded by \(f(x) \ge g(x)\) on \([a,b]\) with uniform density:

\[ M = \rho \int_a^b [f(x) - g(x)]\, dx \qquad \text{(mass)} \]
\[ M_y = \rho \int_a^b x[f(x) - g(x)]\, dx \qquad M_x = \frac{\rho}{2}\int_a^b \left([f(x)]^2 - [g(x)]^2\right) dx \]
\[ \bar{x} = \frac{M_y}{M}, \qquad \bar{y} = \frac{M_x}{M} \]
  • \(M_x\): moment about the \(x\)-axis (controls \(\bar{y}\))
  • \(M_y\): moment about the \(y\)-axis (controls \(\bar{x}\))
  • With uniform density, \(\rho\) cancels in \(\bar{x}\) and \(\bar{y}\) -- the center of mass is purely geometric (centroid)
  • Pappus's Theorem: Volume of solid of revolution = \(2\pi \bar{r} \cdot A\), where \(\bar{r}\) is the distance from the centroid to the axis and \(A\) is the area

6.5 Force, Work, and Pressure

Work (constant force): \(W = Fd\)

Work (variable force):

\[ W = \int_a^b F(x)\, dx \]

Hooke's Law: Spring force \(F = kx\), where \(k\) is the spring constant and \(x\) is displacement from natural length.

\[ W = \int_0^d kx\, dx = \frac{1}{2}kd^2 \]

Work pumping a fluid:

\[ W = \int_a^b \rho g\, A(x)\, d(x)\, dx \]
  • \(\rho\) = fluid density, \(g\) = gravitational acceleration, \(A(x)\) = cross-sectional area of the tank at height \(x\), \(d(x)\) = distance the slice must be lifted

Hydrostatic Force on a vertical submerged plate:

\[ F = \int_a^b \rho g\, d(x)\, w(x)\, dx \]
  • \(d(x)\) = depth of strip below surface, \(w(x)\) = width of plate at depth \(d(x)\)

6.6 Hyperbolic Functions

Definitions:

\[ \sinh x = \frac{e^x - e^{-x}}{2}, \qquad \cosh x = \frac{e^x + e^{-x}}{2}, \qquad \tanh x = \frac{\sinh x}{\cosh x} \]

Key Identity:

\[ \cosh^2 x - \sinh^2 x = 1 \]
