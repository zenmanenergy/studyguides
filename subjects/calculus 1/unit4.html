Calculus 1 — Unit 4: Contextual Applications of Differentiation

Rates of Change: Motion

QuantityNotationMeaning
Position\(s(t)\)Location at time \(t\)
Velocity\(v(t) = s'(t)\)Rate of change of position
Acceleration\(a(t) = v'(t) = s''(t)\)Rate of change of velocity
Speed\(|v(t)|\)Magnitude of velocity
  • Moving right/up when \(v(t) > 0\); left/down when \(v(t) < 0\)
  • Speeding up when \(v\) and \(a\) have the same sign; slowing down when opposite signs
  • At rest when \(v(t) = 0\)

Related Rates

  • Two or more quantities both changing with respect to time
  • Differentiate a geometric or physical equation implicitly with respect to \(t\)
Strategy: Draw diagram → write equation relating variables → differentiate both sides w.r.t. \(t\) → plug in known values and solve
  • Common setups: ladder sliding, balloon expanding, shadow lengthening, water draining
  • Example (expanding circle): \(A = \pi r^2 \;\Rightarrow\; \dfrac{dA}{dt} = 2\pi r \dfrac{dr}{dt}\)
  • Example (Pythagorean): \(x^2 + y^2 = z^2 \;\Rightarrow\; 2x\dfrac{dx}{dt} + 2y\dfrac{dy}{dt} = 2z\dfrac{dz}{dt}\)

Linear Approximation (Linearization)

\[ L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x - a) \] This is the tangent line at \(x = a\), used to approximate \(f(x)\) near \(a\).
  • The closer \(x\) is to \(a\), the better the approximation
  • Example: approximate \(\sqrt{4.02}\) using \(f(x) = \sqrt{x}\) at \(a = 4\):
\(f'(x) = \dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{x}},\quad f'(4) = \dfrac{1}{4}\)
\(L(4.02) = 2 + \dfrac{1}{4}(0.02) = 2.005\)

L'Hopital's Rule

If \(\lim_{x \to c} \dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) gives \(\dfrac{0}{0}\) or \(\dfrac{\infty}{\infty}\), then: \[ \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} \]
  • Can be applied repeatedly if still indeterminate
  • Only valid for indeterminate forms — do not use when denominator limit is nonzero
  • Other indeterminate forms (\(0 \cdot \infty\), \(\infty - \infty\), \(1^\infty\), etc.) must first be rewritten as \(0/0\) or \(\infty/\infty\)
