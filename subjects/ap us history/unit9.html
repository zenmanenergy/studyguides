AP US History — Unit 9: Period 9 (1980–Present)

The Reagan Revolution

  • Reagan elected 1980 on conservative platform: reduce government, cut taxes, fight communism
  • Supply-side economics ("Reaganomics"): cut top marginal tax rates from 70% to 28%; deregulation; belief that wealth "trickles down"
  • Reagan drastically increased defense spending → increased national debt
  • Cold War escalation: Reagan Doctrine — support anti-communist rebels worldwide (Nicaragua, Afghanistan)
  • Iran-Contra Affair: secret arms sales to Iran used to fund Nicaraguan Contras; major scandal
  • Conservative social agenda: "Moral Majority" (Jerry Falwell); opposition to abortion, gay rights

End of the Cold War

  • Gorbachev's reforms: glasnost (openness) and perestroika (restructuring) — opened door to change
  • Fall of Berlin Wall (November 9, 1989); German reunification 1990
  • USSR dissolved December 25, 1991; 15 independent nations emerged
  • George H.W. Bush: managed Cold War's end; Gulf War (1991) expelled Iraq from Kuwait with UN coalition
  • Post-Cold War debates: what is US role in the world? Unipolar moment; "end of history" (Fukuyama)

1990s: Globalization and Culture Wars

  • Clinton presidency: "Third Way" liberal; budget surpluses; NAFTA (1994) — free trade; WTO membership for China
  • Technology boom: internet, dot-com bubble; globalization transformed economy
  • Culture wars: debates over abortion, multiculturalism, LGBT rights, immigration intensified
  • Clinton impeached (1998) over perjury/obstruction related to Monica Lewinsky scandal; acquitted by Senate
  • Welfare Reform Act (1996): "end welfare as we know it"; work requirements; devolved to states
  • Rising income inequality; deindustrialization; Rust Belt decline

9/11 and the War on Terror

  • September 11, 2001: al-Qaeda attacks on World Trade Center and Pentagon; 2,977 killed
  • USA PATRIOT Act (2001): expanded government surveillance; civil liberties concerns
  • Afghanistan War (2001): toppled Taliban; 20-year conflict; US withdrawal 2021
  • Iraq War (2003): Bush claimed WMDs; no WMDs found; destabilized region; cost $2+ trillion
  • Department of Homeland Security created; TSA; surveillance debate (NSA programs)
  • Hurricane Katrina (2005): exposed racial and class inequality in disaster response
