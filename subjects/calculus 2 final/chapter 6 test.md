## Question 1

Set up, but do not evaluate, an integral defining the surface area of the solid obtained by revolving the curve

$$x^2 - 3 = y^2 + 2y, \quad 0 \leq y \leq 2$$

about the $y$-axis.

## Question 2

A force of $50\text{ lb}$ stretches a $9\text{ ft}$ spring by $\dfrac{1}{2}\text{ ft}$. Find the work done in stretching the spring from $11\text{ ft}$ to $14\text{ ft}$. Write the exact answer. Do not round.

---

## Question 3

At a construction site, a $120\text{ ft}$ cable is supporting a piece of equipment that weighs $60\text{ lb}$. In order to lift the equipment by $30\text{ ft}$, the cable is wound on a cylindrical drum. If the cable weighs $5\text{ lb/ft}$, find the work done during this process.

---

## Question 4

Set up, but do not evaluate, an integral defining the arc length of the graph of the equation

$$y = x^5 + 2$$

over the interval $0 \leq x \leq 4$. Then find the corresponding arc length function $s(x)$.

---

## Question 5

Find the volume of the solid that results from rotating the region between the graph of

$$y = 6\sqrt{8 - x^3}$$

and the $x$-axis about the $x$-axis, over the interval $0 \leq x \leq 2$. Write the exact answer. Do not round.

---

## Question 6

Find the centroid of the plane region of constant density bounded by the curves

$$y = x + 1, \quad y(x + 1) = 1, \quad y = 0, \quad \text{and} \quad x = 3.$$

If possible, use symmetry to simplify your calculations. Write your answer as an ordered pair. Write the exact answer. Do not round.

---

## Question 7

Find the volume of the solid that results from rotating the region bounded by the graphs of

$$y = \tfrac{1}{6}x - 3, \quad x = 0, \quad \text{and} \quad y = 0$$

about the $y$-axis. Write the exact answer. Do not round.

---

## Question 8

Use the shell method to find the volume of the solid that is generated by revolving the region bounded by $y = 0$ and the graph of

$$f(x) = 9 - 0.5x$$

about the $x$-axis over the interval $17 \leq x \leq 18$. Write the exact answer. Do not round.

---

## Question 9

A thin rod of length $l = 130\text{ cm}$, with a continuously varying density

$$\rho = 0.4 + 0.07x \ \text{g/cm},$$

is placed into the coordinate system so that it lies horizontally on the $x$-axis with its left endpoint coinciding with the origin. Find its center of mass. Round your answer to four decimal places if necessary.
