AP US History — Unit 8: Period 8 (1945–1980)

Cold War Origins and Foreign Policy

  • Containment doctrine (Truman, 1947): prevent spread of communism; George Kennan's "Long Telegram"
  • Truman Doctrine: US would support free peoples resisting communist subversion (Greece, Turkey)
  • Marshall Plan: $13 billion to rebuild Western Europe; prevent communism through prosperity
  • NATO (1949): collective defense pact; USSR formed Warsaw Pact in response
  • Korean War (1950–53): limited war; ended in stalemate at 38th parallel; first test of containment
  • Nuclear arms race: USSR got the bomb 1949; US developed hydrogen bomb 1952; MAD (Mutually Assured Destruction) doctrine

Postwar Society and McCarthyism

  • Baby Boom (1946–64): 76 million births; suburbs grew (Levittown); interstate highway system (1956)
  • Consumer culture: TV, automobiles, appliances; conformity vs. counterculture tension
  • Second Red Scare: House Un-American Activities Committee (HUAC); Hollywood Ten; loyalty oaths
  • McCarthyism: Senator Joseph McCarthy's reckless accusations; "Are you now or have you ever been..."
  • Rosenberg execution (1953); Army-McCarthy hearings (1954) ended McCarthy's influence

Vietnam War and Social Upheaval

  • Gulf of Tonkin Resolution (1964): LBJ given broad war powers; escalation began
  • US troop levels peaked at 543,000 (1969); 58,000 Americans killed
  • Tet Offensive (1968): massive NVA/Viet Cong attacks; undermined public support despite US military success
  • Kent State (1970): National Guard killed 4 students at anti-war protest; massive campus protests
  • Nixon's Vietnamization: gradual withdrawal; Paris Peace Accords (1973); South Vietnam fell 1975
  • War Powers Act (1973): limited president's ability to commit troops without congressional approval

Great Society and Nixon

  • LBJ's Great Society: Medicare/Medicaid (1965), Elementary and Secondary Education Act, Voting Rights Act, War on Poverty (Head Start, VISTA)
  • 24th Amendment (1964): banned poll tax; Immigration Act of 1965 ended national-origins quotas
  • Nixon (1969–74): detente with China and USSR; SALT I arms limitation; EPA created (1970)
  • Watergate: break-in at DNC HQ; cover-up; Saturday Night Massacre; Nixon resigned Aug 9, 1974
  • Women's liberation: NOW (1966), ERA debate, Roe v. Wade (1973)
  • Stagflation (high inflation + unemployment) + OPEC oil embargo (1973) ended postwar economic prosperity
