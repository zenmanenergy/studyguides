Unit Conversion (Dimensional Analysis)
MethodMultiply by a conversion factor so the unwanted unit cancels.
Cube the factor for cubic units (e.g. µm3 to m3: use (10-6)3).
Scientific notationWrite as a × 10n, where 1 ≤ |a| < 10.
Decimal moves left = positive n; right = negative n.
AccuracyClose to the true/reference value (within ~2%).
PrecisionMeasurements close to each other (within ~5% of each other).

Writing Ionic Formulas
Cross-multiply chargesCation charge becomes anion subscript; anion charge becomes cation subscript. Reduce if needed.
Al3+ + O2- → Al2O3
Polyatomic anion > 1Wrap in parentheses before subscript.
Ca2+ + OH- → Ca(OH)2
Counting Atoms in a Formula
SubscriptsApply to the element immediately to the left.
ParenthesesOutside subscript multiplies everything inside.
Mg3(PO4)2: P=1×2=2, O=4×2=8
Moles, Mass, and Molecules
n = m / M     (moles = grams / molar mass)
N = n × NA    (particles = moles × 6.022×1023)
Molar mass (M)Sum of (atomic mass × count) for every element. Units: g/mol.
g → molDivide mass by molar mass.
mol → gMultiply moles by molar mass.
mol → moleculesMultiply by 6.022×1023.
molecules → molDivide by 6.022×1023.
g → moleculesg → mol (divide by M) → particles (multiply by NA).
Percent by Mass
% element = (mass of element in 1 mol / molar mass of compound) × 100%
Empirical and Molecular Formula
Empirical formula 1. Assume 100 g sample → % becomes grams.
2. Divide each mass by atomic mass to get moles.
3. Divide all mole values by the smallest.
4. Round or multiply to reach whole-number ratios.
Molecular formulan = molecular molar mass / empirical formula molar mass.
Multiply each subscript in the empirical formula by n.
Solutions and Molarity
M = n / V    (mol per liter; convert mL to L)
M1V1 = M2V2    (dilution; moles of solute are constant)
Find VV = n / M
Find nn = M × V
Dilution (add water)V2 = V1 + added volume; concentration decreases.
EvaporationSolute stays; V2 = remaining volume; concentration increases.
Ion Concentrations from Ionic Compounds
Step 1Write the dissociation equation.
e.g. Mg(NO3)2 → Mg2+ + 2 NO3-
Step 2[ion] = compound molarity × stoichiometric coefficient.
[NO3-] = 2 × [Mg(NO3)2]
From gramsg → mol (divide by M) → ion mol (multiply by coeff.) → molarity (divide by V in L).
Dalton's Atomic Theory (4 Postulates)
IMatter is made of indivisible atoms.
IIAll atoms of an element are identical; different elements differ.
IIIAtoms are conserved in reactions (conservation of mass).
IVCompounds form in fixed whole-number ratios (definite proportions).
