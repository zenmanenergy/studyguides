Calculus 1 — Unit 1: Limits and Continuity

Definition of a Limit

\[ \lim_{x \to c} f(x) = L \]
  • The limit exists if and only if the left-hand and right-hand limits both exist and are equal
  • \(\lim_{x \to c^-} f(x) = L\) and \(\lim_{x \to c^+} f(x) = L\) ⇒ limit = L
  • The function does not need to be defined at \(x = c\) for the limit to exist
  • Evaluate limits by direct substitution first; factor/cancel if you get 0/0

Limit Laws

  • Sum/Difference: \(\lim[f \pm g] = \lim f \pm \lim g\)
  • Product: \(\lim[f \cdot g] = \lim f \cdot \lim g\)
  • Quotient: \(\lim[f/g] = \lim f / \lim g\) (provided \(\lim g \neq 0\))
  • Constant: \(\lim[c \cdot f] = c \cdot \lim f\)
  • Power: \(\lim[f^n] = (\lim f)^n\)
Indeterminate forms: \(\frac{0}{0},\ \frac{\infty}{\infty},\ 0 \cdot \infty,\ \infty - \infty\) — must simplify or use L'Hopital's

Squeeze Theorem

If \(g(x) \le f(x) \le h(x)\) near \(c\), and \(\lim_{x\to c} g(x) = \lim_{x\to c} h(x) = L\), then \(\lim_{x\to c} f(x) = L\)
  • Classic use: \(\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin x}{x} = 1\) and \(\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{1 - \cos x}{x} = 0\)
  • Also used for functions bounded by oscillating expressions, e.g. \(x^2 \sin(1/x)\)

Continuity

A function is continuous at \(x = c\) if all three hold:

  1. \(f(c)\) is defined
  2. \(\lim_{x \to c} f(x)\) exists
  3. \(\lim_{x \to c} f(x) = f(c)\)
Discontinuity TypeDescriptionLimit Exists?
Removable (hole)Gap at one point; can be "filled"Yes
JumpLeft and right limits differNo
Infinite (asymptote)Function goes to ±∞No

Infinite Limits and Limits at Infinity

  • Infinite limit: \(\lim_{x \to c} f(x) = \infty\) — vertical asymptote at \(x = c\)
  • Limit at infinity: \(\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = L\) — horizontal asymptote \(y = L\)
Rational functions: divide numerator and denominator by highest power of \(x\).
\(\lim_{x\to\infty} \frac{3x^2 + 1}{5x^2 - 2} = \frac{3}{5}\)
  • If degree of numerator > denominator: limit is ±∞
  • If degree of numerator < denominator: limit is 0
  • If degrees are equal: limit = ratio of leading coefficients

Intermediate Value Theorem

If \(f\) is continuous on \([a, b]\) and \(N\) is any value between \(f(a)\) and \(f(b)\), then there exists \(c \in (a, b)\) such that \(f(c) = N\).
  • Used to prove roots exist: if \(f(a) < 0\) and \(f(b) > 0\), a root exists on \((a,b)\)
  • Requires continuity on the closed interval
