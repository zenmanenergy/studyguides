Calculus 1 — Unit 3: Composite, Implicit, and Inverse Differentiation

Chain Rule

\[ \frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x) \] "Derivative of outer (with inner inside) times derivative of inner"
  • Identify the outer and inner functions first
  • Example: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}[\sin(x^2)] = \cos(x^2) \cdot 2x\)
  • Example: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}[e^{3x}] = e^{3x} \cdot 3\)
  • Chain rule can be nested: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}[\sin^3(x^2)]\) requires chain rule twice

Implicit Differentiation

  • Use when \(y\) is not explicitly solved for in terms of \(x\)
  • Differentiate both sides with respect to \(x\); apply chain rule to any \(y\) term (multiply by \(dy/dx\))
  • Then solve algebraically for \(dy/dx\)
Example: \(x^2 + y^2 = 25\)
\(2x + 2y\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; \dfrac{dy}{dx} = -\dfrac{x}{y}\)
  • Used for circles, ellipses, and any curve not easily expressed as \(y = f(x)\)

  • Combine with chain rule: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}[\arctan(3x)] = \dfrac{3}{1+9x^2}\)

Logarithmic Differentiation

  • Take \(\ln\) of both sides, then differentiate implicitly — useful for variable exponents
Example: \(y = x^x\)
\(\ln y = x \ln x\)
\(\dfrac{1}{y}\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \ln x + 1 \;\Rightarrow\; \dfrac{dy}{dx} = x^x(\ln x + 1)\)
  • Also simplifies products/quotients with many factors
  • General power rule: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}[x^n] = nx^{n-1}\) works for any real \(n\) via logarithmic differentiation
