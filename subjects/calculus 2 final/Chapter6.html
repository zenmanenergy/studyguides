Topic 1 — Surface Area of Revolution

For a curve \(x = g(y)\) revolved about the \(y\)-axis over \([c,d]\), the surface area is \(SA = 2\pi \int_c^d x\sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{dx}{dy}\right)^2}\, dy\).

Question 1

Set up, but do not evaluate, an integral defining the surface area of the solid obtained by revolving the curve \[ x^2 - 3 = y^2 + 2y, \quad 0 \leq y \leq 2 \] about the \(y\)-axis.

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Solve for \(x\).

\(x^2 = y^2 + 2y + 3\), so \(x = \sqrt{y^2 + 2y + 3}\) (positive root, since the surface is in the region \(x \geq 0\)).

Step 2 — Differentiate.

\(\dfrac{dx}{dy} = \dfrac{2y + 2}{2\sqrt{y^2+2y+3}} = \dfrac{y+1}{\sqrt{y^2+2y+3}}\)

Step 3 — Compute \(1 + \left(\dfrac{dx}{dy}\right)^2\).

\(1 + \dfrac{(y+1)^2}{y^2+2y+3} = \dfrac{y^2+2y+3+(y+1)^2}{y^2+2y+3} = \dfrac{2y^2+4y+4}{y^2+2y+3} = \dfrac{2(y^2+2y+2)}{y^2+2y+3}\)

Step 4 — Set up the surface area integral.

\[ SA = 2\pi \int_0^2 \sqrt{y^2+2y+3}\cdot\sqrt{\frac{2(y^2+2y+2)}{y^2+2y+3}}\, dy = 2\sqrt{2}\,\pi\int_0^2 \sqrt{y^2+2y+2}\, dy \]

Surface area \(= 2\sqrt{2}\,\pi\displaystyle\int_0^2 \sqrt{y^2+2y+2}\, dy\)

Topic 2 — Work: Hooke's Law (Springs)

By Hooke's Law, \(F = kx\) where \(x\) is the displacement from natural length and \(k\) is the spring constant. Work done stretching from extension \(a\) to \(b\) is \(W = \int_a^b kx\, dx\).

Question 2

A force of \(50\text{ lb}\) stretches a \(9\text{ ft}\) spring by \(\dfrac{1}{2}\text{ ft}\). Find the work done in stretching the spring from \(11\text{ ft}\) to \(14\text{ ft}\). Write the exact answer. Do not round.

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Find the spring constant.

Using \(F = kx\): \(50 = k \cdot \dfrac{1}{2}\), so \(k = 100\) lb/ft.

Step 2 — Convert lengths to extensions from natural length.

Natural length = 9 ft. Stretching to 11 ft: extension = \(11 - 9 = 2\) ft. Stretching to 14 ft: extension = \(14 - 9 = 5\) ft.

Step 3 — Set up and evaluate the work integral.

\[ W = \int_2^5 100x\, dx = \left[50x^2\right]_2^5 = 50(25) - 50(4) = 1250 - 200 = 1050 \text{ ft-lb} \]

Work \(= 1050\) ft-lb

Topic 3 — Work: Lifting with a Cable

When lifting an object with a heavy cable, both the constant object weight and the decreasing hanging cable weight contribute to the total force. At position \(x\) (feet lifted), \(F(x) = \text{(object weight)} + \text{(cable weight remaining)}\).

Question 3

At a construction site, a \(120\text{ ft}\) cable is supporting a piece of equipment that weighs \(60\text{ lb}\). In order to lift the equipment by \(30\text{ ft}\), the cable is wound on a cylindrical drum. If the cable weighs \(5\text{ lb/ft}\), find the work done during this process.

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Express the total force as a function of displacement.

Let \(x\) = distance the equipment has been lifted, \(0 \leq x \leq 30\).

Weight of equipment: \(60\) lb (constant).

Remaining hanging cable length: \(120 - x\) ft, weighing \(5(120 - x)\) lb.

Total force: \(F(x) = 60 + 5(120 - x) = 660 - 5x\).

Step 2 — Evaluate the work integral.

\[ W = \int_0^{30} (660 - 5x)\, dx = \left[660x - \frac{5x^2}{2}\right]_0^{30} = 660(30) - \frac{5(900)}{2} = 19800 - 2250 = 17550 \text{ ft-lb} \]

Work \(= 17{,}550\) ft-lb

Topic 4 — Arc Length

The arc length of \(y = f(x)\) over \([a,b]\) is \(L = \int_a^b \sqrt{1+[f'(x)]^2}\, dx\). The arc length function from \(a\) to \(x\) is \(s(x) = \int_a^x \sqrt{1+[f'(t)]^2}\, dt\).

Question 4

Set up, but do not evaluate, an integral defining the arc length of the graph of the equation \[ y = x^5 + 2 \] over the interval \(0 \leq x \leq 4\). Then find the corresponding arc length function \(s(x)\).

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Differentiate.

\(y = x^5 + 2 \Rightarrow y' = 5x^4\)

Step 2 — Set up the arc length integral.

\[ L = \int_0^4 \sqrt{1+(5x^4)^2}\, dx = \int_0^4 \sqrt{1+25x^8}\, dx \]

Step 3 — Write the arc length function.

\[ s(x) = \int_0^x \sqrt{1+25t^8}\, dt \]

Arc length \(= \displaystyle\int_0^4 \sqrt{1+25x^8}\, dx\); arc length function \(s(x) = \displaystyle\int_0^x \sqrt{1+25t^8}\, dt\)

Topic 5 — Volume: Disk Method

Revolving \(y = f(x)\) about the \(x\)-axis over \([a,b]\) produces a solid with volume \(V = \pi\int_a^b [f(x)]^2\, dx\). Square the function first before integrating.

Question 5

Find the volume of the solid that results from rotating the region between the graph of \[ y = 6\sqrt{8 - x^3} \] and the \(x\)-axis about the \(x\)-axis, over the interval \(0 \leq x \leq 2\). Write the exact answer. Do not round.

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Apply the disk method formula.

\[ V = \pi\int_0^2 \left[6\sqrt{8-x^3}\right]^2 dx = \pi\int_0^2 36(8-x^3)\, dx \]

Step 2 — Integrate.

\[ V = 36\pi\int_0^2 (8-x^3)\, dx = 36\pi\left[8x - \frac{x^4}{4}\right]_0^2 = 36\pi\left[16 - \frac{16}{4}\right] = 36\pi(16-4) = 36\pi \cdot 12 = 432\pi \]

\(V = 432\pi\)

Topic 6 — Centroid of a Plane Region

For a region of constant density, \(\bar{x} = M_y/A\) and \(\bar{y} = M_x/A\), where \(M_y = \int x\,f(x)\, dx\), \(M_x = \frac{1}{2}\int [f(x)]^2\, dx\), and \(A\) is the area.

Question 6

Find the centroid of the plane region of constant density bounded by the curves \[ y = x + 1, \quad y(x + 1) = 1, \quad y = 0, \quad \text{and} \quad x = 3. \] If possible, use symmetry to simplify your calculations. Write your answer as an ordered pair. Write the exact answer. Do not round.

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Identify the region.

The line \(y = x+1\) passes through \((-1,0)\) and \((0,1)\). The hyperbola \(y = \dfrac{1}{x+1}\) passes through \((0,1)\) and \((3,\tfrac{1}{4})\). Both curves meet at \((0,1)\). No useful symmetry is available.

The region has a composite upper boundary: \(y = x+1\) for \(-1 \leq x \leq 0\) and \(y = \dfrac{1}{x+1}\) for \(0 \leq x \leq 3\), with \(y = 0\) below and \(x = 3\) on the right.

Step 2 — Find the area \(A\).

\[ A = \int_{-1}^0 (x+1)\, dx + \int_0^3 \frac{1}{x+1}\, dx = \left[\frac{(x+1)^2}{2}\right]_{-1}^0 + \bigl[\ln(x+1)\bigr]_0^3 = \frac{1}{2} + \ln 4 \]

Step 3 — Find \(M_y\).

\(\displaystyle\int_{-1}^0 x(x+1)\, dx = \int_{-1}^0 (x^2+x)\, dx = \left[\frac{x^3}{3}+\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_{-1}^0 = 0 - \left(-\frac{1}{3}+\frac{1}{2}\right) = -\frac{1}{6}\)

\(\displaystyle\int_0^3 \frac{x}{x+1}\, dx = \int_0^3 \left(1-\frac{1}{x+1}\right)dx = \bigl[x-\ln(x+1)\bigr]_0^3 = 3-\ln 4\)

\(M_y = -\dfrac{1}{6} + 3 - \ln 4 = \dfrac{17}{6} - \ln 4\)

Step 4 — Find \(M_x\).

\(\dfrac{1}{2}\displaystyle\int_{-1}^0 (x+1)^2\, dx = \dfrac{1}{2}\left[\dfrac{(x+1)^3}{3}\right]_{-1}^0 = \dfrac{1}{2}\cdot\dfrac{1}{3} = \dfrac{1}{6}\)

\(\dfrac{1}{2}\displaystyle\int_0^3 \frac{1}{(x+1)^2}\, dx = \dfrac{1}{2}\left[-\dfrac{1}{x+1}\right]_0^3 = \dfrac{1}{2}\!\left(-\dfrac{1}{4}+1\right) = \dfrac{3}{8}\)

\(M_x = \dfrac{1}{6}+\dfrac{3}{8} = \dfrac{4}{24}+\dfrac{9}{24} = \dfrac{13}{24}\)

Step 5 — Compute the centroid.

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{M_y}{A} = \frac{\dfrac{17}{6}-\ln 4}{\dfrac{1}{2}+\ln 4}, \qquad \bar{y} = \frac{M_x}{A} = \frac{\dfrac{13}{24}}{\dfrac{1}{2}+\ln 4} \]

Centroid: \(\left(\dfrac{17/6-\ln 4}{1/2+\ln 4},\;\dfrac{13/24}{1/2+\ln 4}\right)\)

Topic 7 — Volume: Shell Method about the y-axis

Revolving a region about the \(y\)-axis using cylindrical shells: \(V = 2\pi\int_a^b x\cdot h(x)\, dx\), where \(h(x)\) is the height of the region at each \(x\).

Question 7

Find the volume of the solid that results from rotating the region bounded by the graphs of \[ y = \tfrac{1}{6}x - 3, \quad x = 0, \quad \text{and} \quad y = 0 \] about the \(y\)-axis. Write the exact answer. Do not round.

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Identify the region.

\(y = \tfrac{1}{6}x-3\) crosses the \(x\)-axis at \(x = 18\) and the \(y\)-axis at \(y = -3\). The region is the triangle with vertices \((0,0)\), \((18,0)\), and \((0,-3)\).

For \(0 \leq x \leq 18\), the height from the line up to \(y=0\) is \(0 - \left(\tfrac{x}{6}-3\right) = 3-\tfrac{x}{6}\).

Step 2 — Apply the shell method.

\[ V = 2\pi\int_0^{18} x\!\left(3-\frac{x}{6}\right)dx = 2\pi\int_0^{18}\!\left(3x-\frac{x^2}{6}\right)dx \]

Step 3 — Integrate.

\[ V = 2\pi\left[\frac{3x^2}{2}-\frac{x^3}{18}\right]_0^{18} = 2\pi\!\left[\frac{3(324)}{2}-\frac{5832}{18}\right] = 2\pi[486-324] = 2\pi\cdot 162 = 324\pi \]

\(V = 324\pi\)

Topic 8 — Volume: Shell Method about the x-axis

Revolving a region about the \(x\)-axis using horizontal cylindrical shells: \(V = 2\pi\int_c^d y\cdot L(y)\, dy\), where \(L(y)\) is the horizontal length of the region at height \(y\).

Question 8

Use the shell method to find the volume of the solid that is generated by revolving the region bounded by \(y = 0\) and the graph of \[ f(x) = 9 - 0.5x \] about the \(x\)-axis over the interval \(17 \leq x \leq 18\). Write the exact answer. Do not round.

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Identify the region.

At \(x=17\): \(f(17)=0.5\). At \(x=18\): \(f(18)=0\). The region lies between \(y=0\) and \(y=9-0.5x\) for \(17 \leq x \leq 18\). Maximum height is \(y=0.5\).

Step 2 — Set up horizontal shells.

For a shell at height \(y\) (\(0 \leq y \leq \tfrac{1}{2}\)): solve \(9-0.5x = y\) to get \(x = 18-2y\). The condition \(y \leq f(x)\) requires \(x \leq 18-2y\), so the shell spans \(17 \leq x \leq 18-2y\).

Shell length: \((18-2y)-17 = 1-2y\).

\[ V = 2\pi\int_0^{1/2} y(1-2y)\, dy = 2\pi\int_0^{1/2}(y-2y^2)\, dy \]

Step 3 — Integrate.

\[ V = 2\pi\left[\frac{y^2}{2}-\frac{2y^3}{3}\right]_0^{1/2} = 2\pi\!\left[\frac{1}{8}-\frac{1}{12}\right] = 2\pi\cdot\frac{1}{24} = \frac{\pi}{12} \]

\(V = \dfrac{\pi}{12}\)

Topic 9 — Center of Mass of a Rod

For a rod with variable density \(\rho(x)\) over \([0,l]\): total mass \(m = \int_0^l \rho(x)\, dx\) and center of mass \(\bar{x} = \dfrac{1}{m}\int_0^l x\,\rho(x)\, dx\).

Question 9

A thin rod of length \(l = 130\text{ cm}\), with a continuously varying density \[ \rho = 0.4 + 0.07x \ \text{g/cm}, \] is placed into the coordinate system so that it lies horizontally on the \(x\)-axis with its left endpoint coinciding with the origin. Find its center of mass. Round your answer to four decimal places if necessary.

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Find the total mass \(m\).

\[ m = \int_0^{130}(0.4+0.07x)\, dx = \left[0.4x+0.035x^2\right]_0^{130} = 52 + 0.035(16900) = 52 + 591.5 = 643.5 \text{ g} \]

Step 2 — Find the first moment.

\[ \int_0^{130} x(0.4+0.07x)\, dx = \left[0.2x^2+\frac{0.07x^3}{3}\right]_0^{130} = 0.2(16900)+\frac{0.07(2{,}197{,}000)}{3} = 3380+\frac{153{,}790}{3} = \frac{163{,}930}{3} \]

Step 3 — Compute the center of mass.

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{163930/3}{643.5} = \frac{327{,}860}{3861} \approx 84.9158 \text{ cm} \]

Center of mass \(\bar{x} \approx 84.9158\) cm

Topic 10 — Shell Method: Setting Up a Volume Formula

When the problem asks only for the integral formula, apply \(V = 2\pi\int_a^b x\,f(x)\, dx\) (shells about the \(y\)-axis) and stop before evaluating.

Question 10

Using the shell method, find a formula for the volume of the solid that results when the region bounded by the graphs of the equations \[ y = 28e^{-x} - 1, \quad x = 0, \quad x = \ln(28), \quad \text{and} \quad y = 0 \] is revolved about the \(y\)-axis. Do not evaluate the integral.

Walkthrough

Step 1 — Confirm the region boundaries.

At \(x=0\): \(y = 28e^0-1 = 27\). At \(x = \ln 28\): \(y = 28\cdot\tfrac{1}{28}-1 = 0\). The curve starts at \((0,27)\), decreases to \((\ln 28, 0)\), and the region lies above \(y = 0\).

Step 2 — Apply the shell method about the \(y\)-axis.

Shell at position \(x\): radius \(= x\), height \(= 28e^{-x}-1\).

\[ V = 2\pi\int_0^{\ln 28} x\bigl(28e^{-x}-1\bigr)\, dx \]

\(V = 2\pi\displaystyle\int_0^{\ln 28} x\bigl(28e^{-x}-1\bigr)\, dx\)
