Calculus 1 — Unit 6: Integration and Accumulation of Change

Riemann Sums and the Definite Integral

  • Partition \([a,b]\) into \(n\) subintervals of width \(\Delta x = \dfrac{b-a}{n}\)
  • Left Riemann sum: sample at left endpoint of each subinterval
  • Right Riemann sum: sample at right endpoint
  • Midpoint sum: sample at midpoint of each subinterval
\[ \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(x_i^*)\,\Delta x \]
  • The definite integral equals the net signed area between \(f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis on \([a,b]\)
  • Area below the \(x\)-axis is counted as negative

Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Part 1: If \(F(x) = \displaystyle\int_a^x f(t)\,dt\), then \(F'(x) = f(x)\)

Part 2: \(\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a)\), where \(F' = f\)
  • Part 1: the accumulation function is an antiderivative of the integrand
  • Part 2: use an antiderivative to evaluate definite integrals exactly
  • Chain rule with FTC Part 1: \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\!\int_a^{g(x)} f(t)\,dt = f(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)\)

u-Substitution

  • The chain rule in reverse: let \(u = g(x)\), then \(du = g'(x)\,dx\)
  • Rewrite entire integral in terms of \(u\), integrate, substitute back
Example: \(\displaystyle\int 2x\cos(x^2)\,dx\)
Let \(u = x^2,\; du = 2x\,dx\)
\(= \displaystyle\int \cos(u)\,du = \sin(u) + C = \sin(x^2) + C\)
  • For definite integrals: change the limits of integration to \(u\)-values, or substitute back before evaluating
